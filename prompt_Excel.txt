TRACK 2 — PRODUCT COMPETITORS (Camel Finance type) → Other PRODUCTS (subscriptions, tools, indicators, apps) in the same product category, targeting the same end consumer, in the geography → Found via: TradingView, YouTube, Whop.com, Gumroad, subscription sites, app stores, Telegram channels, Reddit, Instagram, Twitter/X, Discord → Unchanged from V5 — see Part B.

══════════════════════ PART B — HOW TO FIND CAMEL FINANCE-TYPE PRODUCT COMPETITORS ══════════════════════

When the business has a subscription product (indicator, signal service, trading tool, SaaS), the following sources MUST be searched in order to find competing products. These sources are MANDATORY — they cannot be skipped.

── SOURCE PRIORITY 1: TRADINGVIEW PLATFORM ── TradingView is where indicator subscription products live. Search: • tradingview.com/scripts → filter by methodology keywords from the product (e.g., "cycle low", "DCL WCL", "cycle trading", "buy sell arrow", "Bitcoin cycle") • Check the "invite-only" / protected scripts — these are subscription products • Check author profiles for location to confirm geography • Look for scripts with large follower counts or paid/invite-only access • Search: "site:tradingview.com [methodology keyword] [geography]"

── SOURCE PRIORITY 2: YOUTUBE CHANNELS ── YouTube is the primary acquisition channel for trading subscription products. A YouTube channel teaching a trading methodology and selling a subscription is a direct Camel Finance-type competitor. Search: • "[methodology] trading [city/state/country]" • "[asset class] indicator subscription [geography]" • "[cycle trading / signal service] YouTube [geography]" • Check channel About tab for location • Any channel with 1,000+ subscribers AND a paid product/link in description

── SOURCE PRIORITY 3: SUBSCRIPTION MARKETPLACES ── Many trading signal and indicator products are sold on dedicated platforms: • Whop.com — search "[methodology] [geography]" or "[asset] indicator" • Gumroad.com — search "[trading indicator] [geography]" • Payhip.com — search "[trading tool] [country/city]" • Teachable / Thinkific — trading courses with signal components • substack.com — trading newsletters with signal components in the geography

── SOURCE PRIORITY 4: SOCIAL MEDIA (for product discovery) ── Most trading subscription products market on social media before having a website: • Instagram: search "[trading methodology] [city hashtag]" + bio links to products • Twitter/X: search "[indicator] [subscription] [city/state]" in bio • Telegram: search "[trading signals] [geography]" — many signal services live here • Reddit: r/algotrading + geography-relevant subreddits + city filter • Facebook Groups: "[trading] [city name]" groups

── SOURCE PRIORITY 5: APP STORES ── • Google Play: search "[trading signals] [indicator]" + check developer country • Apple App Store: same approach

── SOURCE PRIORITY 6: WEB SEARCH FOR SUBSCRIPTION PRODUCTS ── Use these specific query patterns: • "[methodology] indicator subscription [geography]" • "TradingView indicator [methodology] [city/country]" • "[asset] cycle trading subscription [geography]" • "[buy sell signal] indicator [geography] paid" • "trading signals [city] subscription"

═════════════════════ PART C — WHAT COUNTS AS A "CAMEL FINANCE-TYPE PRODUCT COMPETITOR" ═══════════════════════

A product is a direct Camel Finance competitor if it has ALL of these: ✅ It is a SUBSCRIPTION (monthly/annual fee, not one-time service) ✅ It provides buy/sell signals OR trading indicators OR cycle analysis ✅ It is sold to RETAIL TRADERS (not institutional clients) ✅ It works on TradingView OR delivers signals via app/Telegram/email ✅ It covers financial assets (stocks, crypto, forex, commodities, indices)

A product is an ADJACENT competitor (still include, labelled separately) if: ⚠ It is a trading education course with signals bundled in ⚠ It is a copy-trading service (user follows a trader automatically) ⚠ It is a stock tips / advisory service (regulated adviser registered) ⚠ It is a trading community/Discord with paid signals

EXCLUSIONS (do NOT include as product competitors): ⛔ Enterprise B2B trading platforms (for brokers/institutions) ⛔ Broker platforms that include basic indicators for free ⛔ Generic charting tools (Investing.com, TradingView free tier) ⛔ One-time indicator purchases with no subscription
